One of the easiest ways to reduce security risk is to avoid giving unnecessary access.

The principle of least privilege means every user, service, or application should receive only the permissions required to complete its task. Extra permissions often remain unnoticed until they are abused.

In many environments, users collect access over time. They change roles, move between departments, or temporarily receive elevated permissions that are never removed. Each unused permission becomes another opportunity for mistakes or misuse.

Reviewing permissions regularly is just as important as granting them. Security is not only about adding controls but also about removing what is no longer needed.

The fewer privileges available, the smaller the impact if an account is compromised.
